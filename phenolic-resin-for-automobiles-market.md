<p><ul><li>Thermoplastic Phenolic Resin</li><li>Thermosetting Phenolic Resin</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The automotive market utilizes two main types of phenolic resins: thermoplastic and thermosetting. Thermoplastic phenolic resins are characterized by their ability to be reshaped upon heating, offering flexibility and ease of processing, which is beneficial for parts requiring complex shapes. In contrast, thermosetting phenolic resins undergo a curing process that creates a rigid structure, providing excellent thermal stability and chemical resistance, ideal for high-performance applications such as components exposed to extreme conditions. Both types contribute to lightweight and durable automotive designs.</p></p>
