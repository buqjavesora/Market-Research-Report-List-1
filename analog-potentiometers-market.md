<p><p>The analog potentiometers market is primarily divided into two types: through-hole and SMD (Surface Mount Device) types. Through-hole potentiometers feature longer leads that allow for easy insertion into printed circuit boards, making them ideal for manual assembly and larger designs. In contrast, SMD potentiometers are compact, designed for automated assembly processes, and suitable for high-density applications. Both types serve critical roles in adjusting voltage levels and signal modulation in electronic devices, catering to diverse manufacturing needs and design specifications.</p></p>
